# Executive Summary

## Project
Supply Chain Manager: Supplier OTIF Improvement

## Executive Overview
The business was facing a measurable performance gap: Late supplier deliveries caused production delays, expedited freight costs, and missed customer shipment commitments. The project was launched to create structure, accountability, and measurable improvement across the operating process.

## Objective
Raise supplier OTIF from 82% to 94%, reduce expedited freight by 35%, and cut average supplier response time from 42 hours to 18 hours.

## Strategy
Built supplier scorecards, created escalation SLAs, revised reorder points for critical materials, and launched monthly supplier business reviews.

## Outcome
The project delivered measurable improvement across the core KPIs. The strongest evidence of success was the final KPI dashboard, which shows improved performance versus baseline and target.

## Recommendation
Continue supplier scorecards and move chronic late suppliers to corrective action plans or alternate sourcing.

## Why This Project Matters for Manager Roles
This project demonstrates the ability to diagnose a business problem, organize people around a plan, measure progress, manage risks, and communicate results in a way leaders can act on.
