# Retail Operations Manager: Store Performance Turnaround

## Target Role
Operations Manager

## Industry Context
Retail / quick-service operations

## Business Problem
Store labor cost, shrink, and customer wait times were trending above target while customer satisfaction was declining.

## Project Goal
Reduce labor cost percentage from 18.9% to 16.8%, improve NPS from 52 to 66, cut average wait time from 8.4 to 5.5 minutes, and reduce shrink from 3.8% to 2.6% within 12 weeks.

## Management Approach
Built a weekly operating cadence, redesigned staffing by traffic bands, created a shrink-control checklist, introduced queue-monitoring triggers, and launched scorecards for department leads.

## Results Summary
| KPI | Baseline | Target | Final Result | Status |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---|
| Labor Cost % | 18.9% | 16.8% | 16.5% | Exceeded |
| Avg Wait Time | 8.4 min | 5.5 min | 5.2 min | Exceeded |
| NPS | 52 | 66 | 68 | Exceeded |
| Shrink % | 3.8% | 2.6% | 2.4% | Exceeded |
| Schedule Adherence | 74% | 90% | 91% | Met |

## Manager Skills Demonstrated
- KPI ownership and performance management
- Stakeholder communication and meeting facilitation
- Cross-functional planning and execution
- Risk identification and mitigation
- Process improvement and operational control
- Data-informed decision-making

## Final Business Decision
Approve the new staffing matrix as the permanent model and continue monthly shrink audits.
