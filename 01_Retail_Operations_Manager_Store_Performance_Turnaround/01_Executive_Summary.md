# Executive Summary

## Project
Retail Operations Manager: Store Performance Turnaround

## Executive Overview
The business was facing a measurable performance gap: Store labor cost, shrink, and customer wait times were trending above target while customer satisfaction was declining. The project was launched to create structure, accountability, and measurable improvement across the operating process.

## Objective
Reduce labor cost percentage from 18.9% to 16.8%, improve NPS from 52 to 66, cut average wait time from 8.4 to 5.5 minutes, and reduce shrink from 3.8% to 2.6% within 12 weeks.

## Strategy
Built a weekly operating cadence, redesigned staffing by traffic bands, created a shrink-control checklist, introduced queue-monitoring triggers, and launched scorecards for department leads.

## Outcome
The project delivered measurable improvement across the core KPIs. The strongest evidence of success was the final KPI dashboard, which shows improved performance versus baseline and target.

## Recommendation
Approve the new staffing matrix as the permanent model and continue monthly shrink audits.

## Why This Project Matters for Manager Roles
This project demonstrates the ability to diagnose a business problem, organize people around a plan, measure progress, manage risks, and communicate results in a way leaders can act on.
