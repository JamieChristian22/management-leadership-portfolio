# Executive Summary

## Project
People Operations Manager: New-Hire Onboarding and Retention System

## Executive Overview
The business was facing a measurable performance gap: New hires were missing onboarding milestones, managers had inconsistent check-ins, and 90-day retention was below target. The project was launched to create structure, accountability, and measurable improvement across the operating process.

## Objective
Increase 90-day retention from 81% to 91%, improve onboarding completion from 68% to 95%, and raise new-hire satisfaction from 3.6 to 4.4 out of 5.

## Strategy
Built a 30-60-90 onboarding system with manager checklists, automated reminders, buddy assignments, and weekly completion reporting.

## Outcome
The project delivered measurable improvement across the core KPIs. The strongest evidence of success was the final KPI dashboard, which shows improved performance versus baseline and target.

## Recommendation
Adopt the 30-60-90 onboarding system as standard practice and review new-hire risk flags weekly.

## Why This Project Matters for Manager Roles
This project demonstrates the ability to diagnose a business problem, organize people around a plan, measure progress, manage risks, and communicate results in a way leaders can act on.
