# Executive Summary

## Project
Product Operations Manager: Customer Onboarding Optimization

## Executive Overview
The business was facing a measurable performance gap: New customers were taking too long to activate, creating churn risk and additional support tickets. The project was launched to create structure, accountability, and measurable improvement across the operating process.

## Objective
Increase 30-day activation from 54% to 72%, reduce time-to-first-value from 12 days to 6 days, and cut onboarding-related tickets by 25%.

## Strategy
Redesigned onboarding milestones, added lifecycle emails, introduced a customer health score, and created escalation criteria for at-risk accounts.

## Outcome
The project delivered measurable improvement across the core KPIs. The strongest evidence of success was the final KPI dashboard, which shows improved performance versus baseline and target.

## Recommendation
Keep the milestone-based onboarding model and add health-score alerts to weekly customer success reviews.

## Why This Project Matters for Manager Roles
This project demonstrates the ability to diagnose a business problem, organize people around a plan, measure progress, manage risks, and communicate results in a way leaders can act on.
