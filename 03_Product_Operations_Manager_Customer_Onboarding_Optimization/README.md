# Product Operations Manager — Customer Onboarding Optimization

## Project Overview

This project simulates a real-world customer onboarding optimization initiative led from the perspective of a Product Operations Manager. The project focuses on improving the customer onboarding journey, reducing friction, increasing activation, improving cross-functional handoffs, and creating better visibility into onboarding performance.

The project demonstrates how a Product Operations Manager would analyze customer onboarding gaps, align product, customer success, sales, support, and operations stakeholders, define measurable KPIs, and create a structured improvement plan to increase customer activation and retention.

## Business Problem

The organization was experiencing inconsistent customer onboarding outcomes due to unclear handoffs, manual tracking, delayed follow-ups, limited KPI visibility, and inconsistent communication between sales, product, support, and customer success teams.

As a result, some customers experienced slow time-to-value, missed setup steps, confusion during activation, and lower early engagement. Leadership needed a structured onboarding improvement plan that could standardize the process, improve accountability, and create better reporting around customer onboarding performance.

## Project Goals

- Improve the customer onboarding experience from handoff to activation
- Reduce time-to-value for new customers
- Increase onboarding completion and product activation rates
- Standardize cross-functional handoffs between sales, product, support, and customer success
- Improve KPI visibility for onboarding performance
- Identify risks, bottlenecks, and process gaps before they impact customers
- Create a repeatable onboarding playbook for future customers

## Business Impact

This project demonstrates how a Product Operations Manager can improve customer onboarding by turning a fragmented process into a structured, measurable operating system. The project connects customer journey pain points, KPI tracking, stakeholder alignment, risk management, and repeatable playbooks into one organized improvement plan.

The optimized onboarding process helps reduce time-to-value, improve activation, increase customer satisfaction, reduce support friction, and give leadership clearer visibility into onboarding performance.
